### Connection to Standard Chiral Optics

The corrected optical model maps directly onto standard chiral-medium notation:

\[
\kappa = \chi_{\text{geom}}/(2n), \qquad \Delta n = 2\kappa
\]

with

\[
\chi_{\text{geom}} = \chi_0 \lambda G, \qquad G = \sin^2(\theta/2).
\]

This identifies the geometric susceptibility term with an effective chiral splitting parameter. In the refined toy model, ordinary materials remain near the noise floor, while metamaterial-scale couplings (\(\chi_0 \sim 10^{-3}\)) produce measurable refractive splitting and polarization-dependent beam deviation. This makes chiral metamaterial beam-splitting experiments the most promising first test of the framework.
