## The UV Cutoff: Already Derived

Before discussing what remains open, one thing that appeared
in the open problems list needs to be moved out of it.

The UV completion — the behavior of the framework at extreme
energies, near the Planck scale — is not missing. It is
in the framework already, derived from the same geometric
constraint that governs everything else.

The time string cannot curve arbitrarily fast. There is a
physical limit to how sharply the tensioned time substrate
can bend. That limit is expressed as a gradient constraint:

  |∂_μχ| ≤ π   for all spacetime directions μ

The minimum quantum step is one beat step: Δχ_min = π/30.
Six degrees. The full winding budget is 30 × (π/30) = π.
Saturation — using all 30 steps — means the time string
has reached its maximum curvature. It cannot go further.

This is the natural UV cutoff. Not a mathematical regulator
inserted by hand to cancel infinities. Not a free parameter
chosen to match data. The physical limit at which the geometry
of the time string saturates.

The energy scale at this cutoff is near the Planck scale —
the scale where quantum gravity effects become important.
This is not a coincidence. It is the same physics seen from
two directions: the Planck scale is where quantum field theory
breaks down, and the GBP UV cutoff is where the time string
geometry saturates. They are the same physical event described
in two different languages.

What this means for QFT divergences: the infinities that appear
in quantum field theory calculations are the mathematical
signature of approaching the geometric saturation limit.
Renormalization — the procedure physicists use to remove those
infinities — is, in this geometric picture, curvature
normalization: subtracting the background tension to measure
deviations from the vacuum configuration. The infinities
were never physical. They were the geometry telling you
that you had extrapolated past its limits.

The UV cutoff is derived. Status: D.

## The Black Hole: What the Geometry Tells Us

Black holes are where the UV cutoff and the open problems
meet. And the geometry has more to say about them than
the current literature acknowledges.

**At the horizon.**

As a particle approaches a black hole, the curvature of
spacetime increases. In GBP terms: the tension of the
local time string is being compressed toward T_min — the
floor set by the colorless boundary lanes {1,29}.

T_min / T₀ = GEO_B = sin²(π/15) = 0.04323

This is the same GEO_B that appears everywhere in the
framework: the optical reflection floor, the minimum
winding projection, the boundary of coprime lane space.
The black hole horizon is where the local time string
tension reaches this floor.

At T_min, the winding structure that defines a particle's
identity — the specific lane assignment, the phase
relationships, the Hamiltonian coupling — can no longer
be sustained. The geometry is too compressed. Almost
nothing survives.

Almost nothing. Two winding states remain accessible at
T_min: the colorless boundary lanes {1,29}. These are
the outermost coprime lanes — the minimum non-zero
projection, the floor below which the geometry cannot go.
A particle near the horizon loses almost its entire
Hilbert space. Its local geometric identity — the rich
internal structure of T1, T2, or T3 winding — collapses
down to these two surviving states.

This is not the information paradox. This is the geometry
describing what happens to the field near a black hole.
Almost no Hilbert space. Almost no internal structure.
The particle loses its field — its local dimension —
because the time string is too compressed to sustain
the winding that gives the particle its identity.

The entropy is proportional to area because the surviving
states live at the boundary — the horizon — where T_min
is reached. Each Planck-scale cell on the horizon holds
exactly two accessible states: ln(2) bits. Integrated
over the horizon area: S ∝ A × ln(2) / l_P². The
Bekenstein-Hawking formula falls out of counting the
two colorless boundary states per Planck-scale cell.

The structural form is correct. The cell size that makes
the numbers work is l_P — the Planck length — not l_GBP
(the hadronic scale the framework currently operates at).
Bridging those two scales requires approximately 94
steps of the φ-ladder. That calculation is not yet done.
The structure is right. The numbers require more work.

**Between the horizon and the center.**

Moving inward past the horizon, the curvature continues
to increase. The tension continues to compress. Even the
two colorless boundary lanes begin to lose their structure.
The Hilbert space shrinks toward zero. Winding becomes
impossible. The geometry approaches a state of pure
tension with no room for spatial structure at all.

A particle in this region has almost no internal geometry.
Almost no Hilbert space. It retains mass — the tension
floor enforces a minimum energy — but the rich topological
structure that distinguishes a proton from a neutron from
an electron is gone. Everything has collapsed to the
same two boundary states.

**At the center: the balance point.**

At the exact center of a spherically symmetric black hole,
something qualitatively different happens.

The curvature arrives from all directions simultaneously.
The time string is being compressed from every direction
at once. And at the center — the geometric balance point
of a symmetric mass distribution — the competing
compressions cancel each other. The net gradient is zero.

This is not the same as no curvature. The curvature is
enormous — it is arriving from every direction. But at
the balance point, the vectors cancel. The time string
is under extreme isotropic compression, but the net
directional gradient — the thing that distinguishes
one direction from another, that makes one winding
direction preferred over another — is zero.

In this environment, the winding structure can be
restored. The Hilbert space opens back up. Not because
the tension is low — it is extremely high — but because
the tension is isotropic. Equal in all directions.
No preferred axis. No gradient to break the symmetry.
The Z₃₀* lanes become accessible again, not because
the geometry is relaxed but because the geometry is
perfectly balanced.

This is where information goes.

Not onto the horizon as a hologram. Not out through
Hawking radiation as thermal noise. Into the central
balance point, where the competing curvatures cancel
and the Hilbert space is restored under extreme
isotropic compression.

When the black hole eventually evaporates — when the
mass decreases to the point where T_min can no longer
be sustained at the horizon — the central balance point
collapses outward. The information stored in the
restored Hilbert space at the center is released as
the geometry unwinds. This is not thermal radiation.
It carries the geometric structure of what fell in.

**What this resolves.**

The information paradox — the question of whether
information is destroyed when matter falls into a
black hole — has a geometric answer in this framework:

Information is not destroyed. It retreats to the
central balance point where the competing curvatures
cancel and the Hilbert space is restored. It is
inaccessible from outside the horizon not because
it is gone but because the geometry between the
horizon and the center has collapsed to the
two-state floor, creating an information barrier
that requires crossing the T_min boundary.

This is consistent with the holographic principle
(information encoded on the boundary) but provides
the mechanism: the boundary is where T_min is
reached, the two boundary states are the encoding
alphabet, and the full information is stored
deeper inside where the geometry balances.

**Status of this claim:** H — hypothesis.
The geometric picture is self-consistent and resolves
the structural form of Bekenstein-Hawking entropy.
The numerical match requires the 94-step φ-ladder
UV completion from hadronic to Planck scale.
The central balance point mechanism is new and
has not been independently verified. It is presented
as a strongly motivated geometric conjecture.

and the black hole structure partially resolved (H),
the honest list of open problems is shorter and more

   This is the numerical UV completion. The structure

   isotropic compression restore the full Z₃₀*
   winding structure, or only a subset? What
   determines how much information can be stored?

4. Neutrino mass hierarchy: qualitative mechanism
   only. Σm_ν ~ 58-62 meV predicted (P), not yet
   measured at required precision.

5. Big Bang singularity, inflation, CMB: not addressed.
   The framework begins at the hadronic scale. What
   came before is outside its current scope.

6. Dark matter: removed pending further development.

  QFT divergences = geometric saturation.
