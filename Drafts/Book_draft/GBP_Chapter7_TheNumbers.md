## The Mass Formula in Words

Every particle in the Standard Model has a mass. Some
are light — the electron is about half a MeV, a thousand
times lighter than a proton. Some are heavy — the top
quark is 172,000 MeV, nearly as heavy as a gold atom.
The Standard Model measures these masses and records
them. It does not explain them.

The GBP framework explains them.

The mass of a particle is its projection amplitude onto
the time string — the cost of maintaining a specific
winding configuration against the tension of the
underlying geometry. Heavy particles wind through
high-projection lanes. Light particles wind through
low-projection lanes. The photon has zero net projection
and is therefore massless.

This is Malus's Law — the optical law that governs how
much light passes through a polarizing filter at a given
angle — applied to the winding geometry. The same
formula that tells you how much light a polaroid filter
transmits also tells you how heavy a quark is. Not
analogically. Structurally. The same mathematics,
the same physics, the same formula.

The projection amplitude at winding lane r is:

  P(r) = sin²(rπ/15)

This is the fraction of the time string tension that
a particle at lane r can sustain. Multiply by the
torsion coupling κ₀ — a product of three measured
quark masses, not a free parameter — and apply the
geometric factors of the toroid tier, and you have
the mass.

What follows is what that formula produces.

## The Honest Outlier

Before moving to the second table, one number in Table 1
deserves explanation rather than burial.

Lambda_c(2625) — a charm baryon — is assigned to the
wrong topology branch in the current code. It was originally
placed in the same branch as Lambda_c(2595), producing a
7.132% error. This is not a rounding error or a numerical
issue. It is a physical misassignment: the two particles
were assumed to be HQSS (Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry)
partners, which would put them on the same topology branch,
but they are not.

This was identified as a geometric error — the chirality
assignment was wrong — and confirmed by two independent
papers in the literature, Nieves & Pavao (2019) and
Kawakami & Harada (2018), both published after the GBP
assignment was made. The field caught up to the geometry.

Lambda_c(2625) is excluded from the 0.2498% MAPE figure
precisely because the correct chirality fix has been
identified but not yet implemented in the calculation.
It is listed here for transparency. The error is understood.
The fix is known. It will reduce the MAPE further when
applied.
