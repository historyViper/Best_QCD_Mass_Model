## Notes on Writing Style

**The Hawking model:** Equations almost never appear in main text.
The meaning of the equations is always present.
Readers feel the physics without needing the formalism.
Formalism lives in appendices.

**The Herman model:** Each chapter opens in a human moment —
a physical intuition, a surprise, a moment where the math
said something unexpected. Concepts unpacked from there.

**The voice:** First person for discovery stories.
Neutral for physics explanation.
Never pretend the framework is more complete than it is.
Never pretend it is less surprising than it is.

**One test per chapter:** Can a curious person with no physics
background follow the narrative? Can a physicist find nothing
technically wrong? If both: the chapter works.

**Payoff structure:** The FTL chapter (Ch. 12) lands hardest
because by that point the reader has seen the geometry work
across 54 particles, the wormhole, and the Riemann zeros.
When you tell them the voltage standard runs on FTL correlation
and the geometry explains why that doesn't break causality —
they believe it. That's why it goes here, not in Chapter 3.
